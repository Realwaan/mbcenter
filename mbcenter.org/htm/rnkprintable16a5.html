Eagle Scout Rank

Senior Scout Ideals

  1. Create a post or series of posts on your social media account(s) that demonstrate how you are living by the Scout Promise, Scout Law, Scout Motto, and Senior Scout Slogan. Use your posts to showcase acts of kindness, responsibility, helpfulness, and community service that align with these values.

  2. Prove to your fellow Scouts and Leaders that you are living up to the Senior Scout Code as your personal code of conduct in your daily lives.

  3. Create a series of social media posts that show how you're fulfilling your "Duty to God" through religious activities. Share experiences like attending services, volunteering, or helping in your community, with captions that reflect the values you're embracing.

  4. Together with your fellow Scouts in the Crew and Outfit, plan, organize and conduct a "Worn-Out Flag Disposal Ceremony".

Social Activities

  1. Under the supervison of your Outfit Advisors, together with the members of your Crew and Outfit, plan, organize and conduct a cultural or social activity, either indoors or outdoors, involving members of the opposite sex.

Vocational Activities

  1. Attend and actively participate a Career Orientation Forum, Seminar or Symposium for Students conducted by your School and/or organized by any of your preferred Colleges/Universities and wirte your reflection about this activity.

  2. Discuss with your Outfit Advisor, Teacher, or Guidance Counselor about your strengths, hobbies and interest. Explain to them your 3-Year Vocational / Career Path Plan and seek advice for career path planning.

  3. Show proof that your "Scout is Thrifty" savings account is in good standing and discuss with your parents and teachers how you intend to increase your savings for the the future.

Service Activities

  1. Togther with your Crew members, plan, organize, and conduct an Eagle Scout Service Project. Document the project and its impact, ensuring it contributes meaningfully to the community.

Outdoor and Indoor Activities

  1. Together with your Crew and Outfit, volunteer to plan, organize and lead an emergency preparedness drill in your school and/or community and demonstrate how to transport an injured person and give first aid to simple and complex fratures and severe bleeding.

  2. In consultation with your Outfit Advisor, plan, organize and go for a 3-day and 2-night survival expedition camp in any of the BSP Campsite or Government Accredited Natural Parks and Wildlife area and show proof that you have managed to keep yourself dry, safe, healthy and in good condition. Make an essay about this experience.

  3. Participate in at least one (Council, Regional, or National) Jamboree and document your experience by creating a series of social media posts and reflect.

  4. Earn the following Merit Badges:

    1. Life Saving

    2. Global Citizenship

    3. In addition, earn three (3) Career Elective Merit Badges
