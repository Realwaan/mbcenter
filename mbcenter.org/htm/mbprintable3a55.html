Navigation

  1. Do the following:
    1. Discuss with your Outfit the cardinal and sub-cardinal points. Identify the landmarks located in each direction based on your home as a point of reference
    2. Identify and explain the parts and functions of the compass
    3. Demonstrate the proper use of a compass

  2. Demonstrate first aid procedure for the following types of injuries that could occur while traveling: blisters, cuts, scrapes, animal/insect bites, heat and cold reactions (sunburn, heatstroke, heat exhaustion, hypothermia), dehydration.

  3. Do the following:
    1. Discuss with your counselor the different types of maps
    2. Explain how topographic maps show terrain
    3. Name and point out 10 symbols on a topographic map
    4. Demonstrate how to measure distances on a map

  4. Discuss the importance of "Leave No Trace" principles while navigating.

  5. Do one (1) of the following:
    1. Demonstrate techniques on how to stay calm and focused when lost or facing navigation difficulties
    2. Conduct a 1-km navigation walk (with approval of your counselor) practicing mindfulness exercises such as deep breathing, and focused attention

  6. Discuss with your counselor how navitation map reading and compass use have influenced different cutlures and religions.
