Signaling

  1. Send and receive a message in the International Morse Code, by buzzer or other sound device, at a rate of at least five (5) words per minute.

  2. Show your ability to send and receive a message in the International Morse Code by wigwag, blinker or other light signaling device, at the rate of at least five (5) words per minute.

  3. Know the proper application of the International Morse Code; when, where, and how it can be used to best advantage.

  4. Describe briefly various common codes and methods of signaling which are in use today.

  5. Within a period of three (3) months, log 15 radio contacts showing details of date, time, call sign, signal strength, readability, and location.

  6. Discuss your experience in Radio Scouting communication with the Merit Badge Counselor, including:
    1. Rules and regulations affecting radio communications
    2. Nature of Radio Scouting communication both in local and foreign contacts
    3. Basic theories of how a radio transceiver works
